About Swami Vivekananda

Swami Vivekananda (1863â€“1902) was an Indian monk who played a key role in introducing Indian philosophies of Vedanta and Yoga to the Western world. He is remembered as a great thinker, spiritual leader, and advocate for universal brotherhood and selfless service.

He was born as Narendranath Datta in Kolkata, India, and his teachings have inspired millions around the world. Swami Vivekananda emphasized the importance of education, spiritual development, and service to humanity. He represented India at the 1893 Parliament of the World's Religions in Chicago, delivering a powerful speech that gained global recognition.

Admirable Qualities

Swami Vivekananda's teachings inspire us to live with courage, compassion, and purpose. His vision of unity, strength, and service continues to resonate with people across the globe. Here are some of his most admirable qualities:

  • Unwavering faith in the power of the human spirit
  • Advocate for universal tolerance and harmony
  • Commitment to education and self-improvement
  • Inspiration for social change and empowerment
